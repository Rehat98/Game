Frequently asked questions

Everything you might want to know about how Pictok works.

The basics

What is Pictok?

A daily emoji-decode puzzle game for iOS. Each day you get one new puzzle: a string of emojis where each emoji represents a word. Guess the answer hangman-style by tapping letters.

How do I play?

Look at the emojis. Try to figure out what word or phrase each one decodes to. Some are literal — 🐱🐟 = CATFISH. Some are rebus puns — 🐝🍃 = BELIEF (bee + leaf → "be-lief"). Tap letters to guess; you get five hearts before the puzzle locks.

Is it free?

Yes. No ads, no in-app purchases, no premium tier. Free forever as long as the App Store will let us host it.

Why only one puzzle a day?

Because the daily ritual is the point. Endless content turns puzzle games into time-suck slot machines. One puzzle a day means you finish, you feel done, you come back tomorrow with fresh eyes. The math works: 60 puzzles in v1 = about two months of content.

Gameplay rules

What happens when I run out of hearts?

The puzzle locks for today. You'll see the correct answer and your streak resets to 0. Tomorrow's puzzle is still waiting for you.

How do hints work?

You can use exactly one hint per puzzle. There are two flavors:

  • Reveal subcategory — shows a small detail about the answer (e.g., "Movie · 2008"). Costs 1 heart.
  • Reveal a letter — fills in one letter of the answer for you. Costs 2 hearts.
Pictok is stingy with hints on purpose — the satisfaction comes from cracking it yourself.

How does the streak work?

Solve a puzzle today, your streak goes up by 1. Skip a day OR fail a puzzle, your streak resets to 0. You get one free "streak freeze" each week that auto-rescues you if you miss exactly one day.

Why didn't I get my streak freeze?

You only get one per week, and it only works if you miss exactly one day. If you skip two consecutive days, the freeze can't save you.

When do my hearts refill?

One heart every 4 hours, capped at 5. If you burn through all 5 in the morning, you'll have 1 again by mid-afternoon.

Notifications & reminders

When does the daily notification fire?

9:00 AM your local time, every day. If you've already solved today's puzzle before 9 AM, the notification is suppressed.

How do I turn off notifications?

iOS Settings → Notifications → Pictok → toggle off. Pictok doesn't have an in-app toggle in v1 to keep the app minimal.

Why didn't I get a notification today?

A few possibilities: you already solved today's puzzle (suppression by design), iOS Focus mode is on, or you never granted permission. Pictok only asks for permission after your first solved puzzle.

Will my friends see the answer?

No. The share card never includes the answer — only your puzzle number, category, hearts remaining, and streak.

Privacy & data

Do you collect any data about me?

No. Pictok has no analytics, no tracking, no third-party SDKs, no servers. Everything lives on your device. Read the full privacy policy.

Where is my streak stored?

In iOS UserDefaults — Apple's standard local key-value store. Deleting the app deletes your streak. Restoring an iCloud device backup may bring it back if Apple's backup includes UserDefaults for Pictok.

Can I play offline?

Yes. Pictok never makes a network call. The 60 puzzles ship inside the app and play locally.

Puzzles & content

Where do the puzzles come from?

All 60 v1 puzzles were hand-authored and reviewed for quality. Every emoji is supposed to decode to a word in the answer — either literally or as a rebus pun. If a puzzle feels arbitrary, that's a bug — please tell us.

What happens after the 60 puzzles run out?

A future update (v1.1 or later) will add another batch. We're considering an LLM-assisted pipeline that lets us scale content faster while keeping the quality bar.

A puzzle's answer feels arbitrary / wrong / culturally narrow. What do I do?

Please report it — same email above. We'll review and pull it if the complaint holds up. Future versions will let players flag puzzles directly inside the app.

Technical / device stuff

What iOS version do I need?

iOS 17 or later. Pictok uses some newer SwiftUI features that don't exist on older versions.

Is there an iPad / Android / Web version?

Not yet. v1 is iPhone-only. iPad runs the iPhone layout. Android and Web are not on the roadmap; they'd basically be a separate app.

I deleted the app — can I get my streak back?

If iOS automatically restored your device from an iCloud backup that includes Pictok, possibly yes. Otherwise no — streaks live only on the device. Sorry.

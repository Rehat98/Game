Long description (~150 words)

Pictok takes the daily-puzzle ritual that Wordle made famous and points it at emojis. Every day, one new puzzle drops: a string of two to four emojis representing a movie, song, book, brand, or celebrity. Each emoji decodes to a word in the answer — sometimes literally (🐱🐟 = CATFISH), sometimes as a rebus pun (🐝🍃 = BELIEF, "bee-leaf"). You guess hangman-style, with five hearts to spare for wrong letters.

Pictok is deliberately minimal: 60 hand-authored puzzles, no in-app purchases, no ads, no analytics, no servers, no accounts. Everything stays on your device. Build your streak, share spoiler-free results, then put your phone down until tomorrow. The whole game session is designed to last 30 seconds to 2 minutes.

Key features

  • One new emoji puzzle every day
  • Hand-authored content — 60 puzzles in v1, more in updates
  • Hangman-style letter guessing with 5 hearts per puzzle
  • Streak tracking with a weekly auto-freeze if life happens
  • Spoiler-free share card for posting wins to chats and socials
  • Daily 9 AM local reminder (optional, asked after first solve, not on launch)
  • Local-only stats: streak history, guess distribution, win rate
  • Three difficulty bands rotating across the week
  • Zero data collection. Zero ads. Zero accounts.

What makes Pictok different

The daily puzzle category is dominated by word games where letters are the puzzle (Wordle, Spelling Bee, Connections). Pictok flips the relationship — the emojis are the puzzle, and you use letters to spell out the answer. The rebus-pun mechanic (bee + leaf = BE-LIEF) creates a uniquely satisfying "oh!" moment that pure letter-guessing doesn't.

It's also a deliberate pushback against modern mobile-game design. No daily quests. No premium currency. No "watch ad for hint" loops. No accounts. The app is built so that shipping minimum, then iterating is the philosophy, not "monetize from day one."

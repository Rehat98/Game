# Pictok Privacy Policy

**Last updated: [YOUR_LAUNCH_DATE]**

Pictok is built so that there is essentially nothing to write a long privacy policy about. Here's the entire story.

## What we collect

**Nothing.** Pictok runs entirely on your device. We don't have a server. We don't have a database. We don't have accounts. We can't see your puzzles, your streak, your guesses, or anything else.

## What's stored on your device

The app uses Apple's standard `UserDefaults` to remember:

- Your current and longest streak
- Your lifetime solve and play counts
- The state of today's puzzle (so you can put your phone down and pick it back up)
- Your remaining hearts and when they last refilled

This data never leaves your device. Deleting the app deletes this data. Restoring an iCloud backup of your device may restore it if iOS includes `UserDefaults` in your backup.

## Notifications

If you grant notification permission, Pictok schedules **one local notification per day, on your device** to remind you to play. No remote push service is involved. Apple does not see the content of the notification.

You can turn notifications off at any time in iOS Settings → Notifications → Pictok.

## Third-party services

Pictok uses zero third-party SDKs, analytics providers, advertising networks, or crash reporters. There is no Firebase, no AppsFlyer, no Adjust, no Sentry, no Crashlytics, no Mixpanel, no anything.

## Children's privacy

Pictok is rated 4+ on the App Store and is safe for children. Since we collect no data, there is no child-specific data to protect or delete.

## Changes to this policy

If we ever add a feature that involves sending data off-device (e.g., a future global leaderboard), we'll update this policy and surface a clear opt-in inside the app. We will never silently start collecting data.
